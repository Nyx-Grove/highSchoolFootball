The Red Wolves are intersted in gaining students from ages of 10-13 to recruit for their summer flag football league.

Red Wolves football has been around since the inception of the league in 1929 by founder Joseph J. Tomlin as a four-team conference in Northeast Philadelphia. since then, participation has steadily increased to today's record numbers. Over 1,500 youths participated in high school-sanctioned football programs in 2000, and those numbers are continuing to grow.

SAFETY FIRST Leagues have options with how to structure their program. While leagues can continue the current structure where a player is placed in a division based on his or her age and weight, they may also instead choose to register players based on age only. Currently, an estimated 75-80% of youth football nationally follows an age-only structure. The Red Wolves place value on safety measures that make a difference.
